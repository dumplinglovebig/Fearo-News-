NTLMv1 Group Policy Bypass Revealed Researchers have identified a way to bypass Microsoft's Group Policy designed to disable NTLMv1 authentication, exposing organizations to potential attacks through misconfigured on-premises applications.

NTLMv1 Deprecated: Although officially removed in Windows 11 (24H2) and Server 2025, NTLMv1 remains exploitable due to legacy support and misconfigurations. The Bypass: A setting in the Netlogon Remote Protocol (MS-NRPC) allows NTLMv1 authentication through the NETLOGON_LOGON_IDENTITY_INFO structure, overriding Group Policy settings. Attacker Advantage: This flaw enables threat actors to exploit NTLMv1 vulnerabilities, such as relaying authentication information to gain unauthorized access to sensitive data or perform malicious actions on behalf of victims. Recommendations: Enable audit logs for all NTLM authentication in the domain. Regularly review applications to identify those misconfigured to allow NTLMv1. Keep systems up-to-date and apply stricter authentication protocols like NTLMv2 or Kerberos.
